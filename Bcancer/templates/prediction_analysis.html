Breast Cancer Classification Analysis

Understanding the Key Features in Predicting Malignant and Benign Tumors

Selected Features & Their Thresholds

Feature Benign Range Malignant Range
Mean Radius 10 - 15 mm 17 - 25 mm
Mean Texture 12 - 20 20 - 30
Mean Perimeter 50 - 85 mm 100 - 150 mm
Mean Area 400 - 700 800 - 2500
Mean Smoothness 0.08 - 0.1 0.12 - 0.16

Why These Features Matter?

1. Mean Radius: Malignant tumors tend to have a larger radius, indicating aggressive growth.

2. Mean Texture: Higher texture values in malignant cases suggest irregularity in tissue structure.

3. Mean Perimeter: Malignant tumors have larger perimeters due to abnormal cell growth.

4. Mean Area: The total affected tissue area is significantly higher in malignant tumors.

5. Mean Smoothness: Smoother edges are often associated with benign tumors, while rougher edges indicate malignancy.
